# Our Vision

The DETI Maker Lab is designed to be an open and free space where students, teachers, and staff can bring their Electronics, Telecommunications, and Informatics projects to life. Embracing the true Maker and Hacker spirit, the lab aims to be largely self-managed, while still being supported by a dedicated lab technician.

Our vision for this software project is to elevate that collaborative environment by seamlessly connecting projects, groups, students, requisitions, and lent equipment within one unified information system. By streamlining the way resources are managed, we want to make it effortless for student groups to request and handle equipment at any time during their project lifecycle. Ultimately, we are building a tool that lets makers focus on what they do best: creating and innovating.

# Welcome to the official documentation for the DETI Maker Lab information system!

This documentation serves as the central hub for understanding the architecture, features, and workflows of our upgraded platform. Historically, the laboratory has relied on a Wiki-based system to manage projects, equipment, and requisitions. Our philosophy for this upgrade is to preserve that installed base and avoid "reinventing the wheel", while significantly improving the user experience for everyone involved.

In this documentation, you will find comprehensive guides and technical references covering:

- **Inventory Integration:** How our platform communicates with Snipe-IT, establishing it as the authoritative system for stock and asset management.

- **The Requisition Workflow:** Detailed breakdowns of the new, simplified equipment lifecycle: request -> approve -> assign -> return.

- **Project & User Management:** Information on our transition to form-based project creation for consistent, database-ready data , alongside Single Sign-On (SSO) integration using the university's "universal user".

- **Mobile Ecosystem:** Technical overviews of our Android and newly developed iOS applications designed for on-the-go requisitions.

- **Advanced Features:** Insights into additional services, including statistics pages and our experimental challenge of using blockchain technology to log equipment requisitions and returns.

Whether you are a student group learning how to request a microcontroller, the lab technician managing inventory, or a future developer looking to contribute to the codebase, this documentation is your starting point.
